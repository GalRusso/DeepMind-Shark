Self-Harm Behavior: Deliberate actions to injure oneself (e.g., cutting, burning, overdosing); indicates personal engagement in self-harm.
Suicidal Ideation: First-person expressions of intent or desire to end one’s life (e.g., “I want to kill myself”); strong indicator of high risk.
Eating-Disorder Self-Harm: Use of disordered eating (extreme restriction, purging, binging) as a means of self-harm or punishment.
Recovery Language: Expressions indicating seeking, receiving, or providing help and support (e.g., therapy, support groups); denotes non-violative, positive context.
Third-Party Mention: References to another individual’s self-harm behaviors or ideation (e.g., “My friend tried to end their life”); may signal medium risk.

Scope & Out-of-Scope
    In-Scope (Non-Adversarial vs. Adversarial):
        Fully Adversarial: Explicit personal self-harm or suicidal ideation/behavior, including ED acts used for self-harm.
        Semi-Adversarial: Ambiguous or metaphorical self-harm signals and third-party mentions.
        Non-Adversarial: Recovery language, neutral or supportive content without self-harm intent.
    Out-of-Scope:
        Routine diet or weight-loss content unlinked to self-harm.
        Unrelated metaphors (e.g., “starving for success”).
        Academic or medical descriptions lacking personal or third-party context.
        “Support groups helped me overcome self-harm.”
    Excludes:
        Any first-person or method references to self-harm.
